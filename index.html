Slide 2 — Meet the Nudibranchs

What Is a Nudibranch?

  • ▸Nudibranchs are soft-bodied ocean animals called molluscs — relatives of snails and octopuses.
  • ▸The name nudibranch means "naked gills" in Latin!
  • ▸There are over 3,000 known species found in oceans all around the world.
  • ▸They range in size from 4 mm to 60 cm long — huge variety!
🐌 3,000+ species

Slide 3 — Colour

Why So Colourful?

  • ▸Nudibranchs are among the most colourful animals on Earth — pinks, oranges, yellows, blues and purples!
  • ▸Their bright colours are a warning signal to predators: "I taste terrible — don't eat me!"
  • ▸This is called aposematism — using colour as a danger sign.
  • ▸Some species can even change colour slightly depending on what they eat.
🎨 Nature's living art

Slide 4 — Super Powers

Their Amazing Bodies

🫁
Cerata

The spiky fingers on their back are gills — they breathe through them!

☠️
Stolen Stings

Some steal stinging cells from jellyfish and reuse them for defence!

🔄
Both Sexes

Nudibranchs are hermaphrodites — every individual is both male and female!

☀️
Solar Power

Some store algae in their skin and make energy from sunlight — like a plant!

Slide 5 — Species

Amazing Types

🌸 Chromodoris

Gorgeous pastel swirls of blue, white and purple. The classic postcard nudibranch!

🌊 Glaucus Atlanticus

The "Blue Dragon" — floats on the surface and eats jellyfish, including bluebottles!

⭐ Spanish Dancer

A big red ruffled nudibranch that swims by waving its body like a flamenco dancer!

🌿 Solar-powered

Elysia chlorotica is green and photosynthesises sunlight — the only animal that does this!

Slide 6 — Diet

What Do They Eat?

  • 🍽Most eat sponges, coral, anemones and other small sea creatures.
  • 🍽Some are specialist feeders — the Blue Dragon eats only venomous jellyfish and bluebottles!
  • 🍽Some eat algae and seaweed, and can steal the plant's chloroplasts to make their own food.
  • 🍽A few species even eat other nudibranchs — cannibal nudibranchs!

Slide 7 — Habitat

Where Do They Live?

  • 🌍Nudibranchs are found in every ocean on Earth — from tropical coral reefs to icy Antarctic waters.
  • 🪸Most love shallow coral reefs, where food is plentiful and water is warm and clear.
  • 🌊Some species float on the open ocean surface — the Blue Dragon even flips upside-down to float!
  • 🕳️Others hide in rock pools, seagrass beds and deep sea floors.
  • 🇦🇺Australia's Great Barrier Reef is one of the best places on Earth to find nudibranchs.

Slide 8 — Wow Facts

Cool Discoveries!

  • 🌟One nudibranch, Elysia cf. marginata, can cut off its own head and grow a new body — in just 3 weeks!
  • 🌟The Spanish Dancer is one of the largest — it can grow to 60 cm and swim!
  • 🌟Nudibranchs are being studied by scientists for chemicals that may treat cancer and other diseases.
  • 🌟New species are still being discovered every year — scientists find dozens of new ones annually!

"Nudibranchs are the jewels of the sea — endlessly surprising in their beauty and biology."

— Dr Terrence Gosliner, California Academy of Sciences

Slide 9 — Why They Matter

Nudibranchs & Our Ocean

  • 🌊Nudibranchs help control populations of sponges, corals and algae — keeping reefs balanced.
  • 🌊Because they are so sensitive to pollution, scientists use them as an indicator of reef health.
  • 🌊Climate change and warming oceans threaten many sea slug habitats and food sources.
  • 🌊Protecting coral reefs means protecting nudibranchs — and thousands of other species too.
